On why this exists.

Loneliness is not a meme.

It is a measurable health crisis. The U.S. Surgeon General has compared social isolation, in mortality terms, to smoking up to fifteen cigarettes a day — a 29% increase in the risk of premature death.

25% of young US men report feeling lonely "a lot of the day." That is the largest gap between young men and the rest of the developed world.

Five times as many men today report having no close friends at all than in 1990 — a number that has gone from 3% to 15% over a single generation.

And 38% of dating-app users developed symptoms consistent with clinical depression over the course of one twelve-week study.

The problem isn't you. The problem is that nobody has ever shown you the math on what's actually working in your photos and what isn't.

How it works.
  1. i.

    You upload your photos and any bio screenshots.

    We figure out which is which.

  2. ii.

    You tell us which platform you're on.

    Tinder, Hinge, Bumble — twelve supported. Or pick up to four at once.

  3. iii.

    We grade everything against published research.

    Photofeeler-style dimensions for photos. Red-flag detection for bios. A bucketing pass for prompts.

  4. iv.

    You get a short list of fixes.

    Ranked by estimated match-rate lift. Each one cited. Plus, optionally, a bio rewrite — Coach voice if you want warmth, Roast if you want teeth.

What the engine does, in full.
  • Per-platform scoring across twelve apps. Each platform has its own weighted rubric — your Tinder lead photo is graded differently from your Hinge lead, because the data says it should be.
  • Photofeeler-style photo grading. Six dimensions per photo. Plus 24 detected boolean flags (sunglasses, mirror selfie, fish, exotic car, group photo, full-body, et cetera).
  • Multi-platform best-photo-picker. Upload up to twenty. Get the recommended six for Bumble, nine for Tinder, six for Hinge — in one pass, with swap-in alternates.
  • Bio and prompts breakdown. Per-prompt verdict, specificity flag, one-line critique. Plus a polished rewrite that fixes everything flagged.
  • Coach or Roast voice. Same diagnosis, different delivery. Coach is constructive. Roast is witty with teeth — never cruel, never punching down.
  • A separate Fuck-Boy Detector. A discrete 0–100 score that flags red-flag patterns women told researchers they swipe left on. Quietly, in case you'd rather not look.
  • A multiplier, not a vibe. Every score converts to an estimated match-rate multiplier — "Bumble: 84/100" reads as "approximately three times your baseline match rate."
  • Save and share. Every analysis is saved automatically. Send a friend the verdict, or come back to compare a new run against an older one.
